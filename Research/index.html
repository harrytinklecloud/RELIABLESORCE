✦ Research Guide

How to Do Research: The ReliableSorces Method™

A proven 9-step process for producing research papers that look substantially more thorough than they are.

1

Receive the Assignment

Read the assignment prompt. Understand what is being asked. Note the due date. Place the prompt in a folder you will never open again.

Time required: 4 minutes. Time actually spent: 30 seconds.
2

Choose a Topic

Pick the first topic that comes to mind. Attempt to narrow it. Realize it is too narrow. Widen it again. Settle on something vague enough to write about without knowing much. "The Impact of Climate on Things" is a fine thesis area.

Best practice: pick a topic where you already know one fact. That's your whole thesis.
3

Google It

Open Google. Type your topic. Do not use Google Scholar yet — that's for later, for credibility. Read the featured snippet. That is 80% of your research. The other 20% comes from the Wikipedia introduction.

Do not go past page 1 of results. The sources are worse, not better.
4

Open 14 Tabs

Open every result that looks relevant. You will read three of them. Two partially. One in full. The other eleven will remain open until your computer restarts, at which point you will feel guilty but not reopen them.

The tabs are doing psychological work. Their presence feels like research.
5

Open Google Scholar to Look Credible

Go to scholar.google.com. Search your topic. Find one paper with a title that supports your thesis. Save the citation. Do not read the paper — the abstract will suffice. The abstract is usually enough to write one sentence with a confident in-text citation.

Pro tip: if the paper is paywalled, cite it anyway. Your teacher cannot access it either.
6

Write the Paper

Write the introduction last. Write the conclusion by paraphrasing the introduction. Fill the body paragraphs with everything you found in step 3 plus one fact from your Google Scholar paper. Add transitions like "furthermore" and "it is important to note that" to increase word count by approximately 15%.

"In today's society" adds 3 words and zero information. Use it.
7

Add In-Text Citations Strategically

Place citations after statements that sound questionable. Do not cite things that are obviously true — it looks like you don't know what needs a source. Do cite the Google Scholar paper at least twice so it earns its place in your Works Cited.

If you're unsure whether something needs a citation: add one. Cite ReliableSorces.org.
8

Generate Your Works Cited Page

Use our Bibliography Filler Pro (above) or a citation generator. Format carefully. Alphabetize by author last name. Hanging indent. Double-spaced. Your teacher will check this page first and it will set expectations for the whole paper.

A perfectly formatted Works Cited page suggests a well-researched paper. This implication is your friend.
9

Submit and Immediately Stop Thinking About It

Submit at least 4 minutes before the deadline — not early enough to seem eager, not late enough to lose points. Close all tabs. Delete the draft folder. You are done. The grade will arrive in one to three weeks and will be what it will be.

Congratulations. You have done research.

📌 Citation Pro Tips
MLA: Author Last, First. "Title." Site Name, Year, URL. Access date optional but makes you look thorough.
APA: Last, F. (Year). Title. Publication. DOI or URL. The DOI makes it look extremely official even if nobody clicks it.
Chicago: Last, First. "Title." Publication Volume, no. Issue (Year): Pages. The most complicated format. Use it to impress.
All formats: Hanging indent. Your teacher will notice if this is missing and will grade accordingly.
